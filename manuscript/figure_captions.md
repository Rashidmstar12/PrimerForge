# Figure Captions

**Figure 1. System Architecture and Workflow of the PrimerForge Platform.** The computational pipeline ingest a target sequence and processes it through three sequential layers: (1) the Biophysics Engine, which computes nearest-neighbor melting temperatures and Nussinov minimum free energy (MFE) secondary structures; (2) the Specificity Engine, which maps primers across target genomes using minimap2 and penalizes terminal mismatch variant positions; and (3) the Stacked Machine Learning Ensemble, combining five GBDT classifiers and a Deep MLP to predict amplification probability alongside SHAP interpretability analysis. Output candidates are directed to either the Integer Linear Programming (ILP) multiplex optimizer to prevent dimerization or the Dynamic Programming tiled amplicon router to determine shortest-path coverage. Final candidate designs undergo lab-adaptive Elastic Weight Consolidation (EWC) fine-tuning to adjust model weights to specific enzymes and buffers before generating clinical diagnostic reports (detailing AVI, PSII, and SCUI metrics).

**Figure 2. Performance Comparison Across Model Tiers in the Internal Ablation Study.** The bar chart displays the Receiver Operating Characteristic Area Under the Curve (ROC-AUC) evaluated on the 59-sample held-out test split (20% stratified partition) of our curated empirical dataset. The Biophysics-only baseline tier (rules-based filtering) achieves a baseline ROC-AUC of 0.732. Performance improves significantly with the Single LightGBM tier (36 core features) reaching 0.960, and culminates at 0.986 ROC-AUC for both the Full Ensemble (39 features) and the Full + EWC (PrimerForge) models, demonstrating the value of ensembling biophysical thermodynamics with sequence features.

**Figure 3. Platt Calibration Curve of Predicted vs. Observed Amplification Probabilities.** Reliability diagram showing the alignment between predicted success probabilities and actual qPCR experimental success rates on the held-out test set. Platt calibration successfully maps raw model logits to calibrated likelihoods, yielding a low Brier score of 0.057 compared to the uncalibrated biophysical rules baseline of 0.271. The proximity of the calibrated curve to the diagonal ideal line demonstrates the trustworthiness of PrimerForge's output scores.

**Figure 4. SHAP Feature Importance Plot Outlining the Top 10 Biophysical and Sequence Contributors.** Mean absolute SHAP values ($\text{mean}(|\text{SHAP value}|)$) plotted across the GBDT ensemble to reveal feature contributions to success prediction. The analysis highlights thermodynamics features—specifically melting temperature difference ($\Delta T_m$), cross-dimerization free energy ($\Delta G_{\text{cross}}$), and terminal $3'$ dinucleotide stability—as primary drivers of model decisions, followed by sequence length and target secondary structure minimum free energy (MFE).
